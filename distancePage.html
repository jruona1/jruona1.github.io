Bell Lap

If you're a distance runner, you've found the finish line. This page is your home for weeks of training, aprroved by divison one athletes and coaches alike. The training plan below spans one month, and while you may not see great strides in improvement during this month (such is the nature of our sport), you will notice yourself feeling much stronger and better equipped to handle any race. The training below serves as an introduction to the program for 800/1600 meter runners. Once completed, fill out the form below to get in contact with us so we can send you a personalized training cycle for your next month. At the end of the day, it's all just trials of miles and miles of trials. Happy running!

Created by John Ruona
Mileage Percentage Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday
Week one: 70% mileage Easy run and strides Workout: Speed endurance (4x200m at race pace) Medium-long distance run Easy run and strides Workout: Interval training (6x800m at 5k race pace) Long run: 60-90 minutes at a comfortable pace Rest day
Week two: 75% mileage Easy run and strides Workout: Hill repeats (8-10x30s uphill) Medium-long distance run Easy run and strides Workout: Tempo run (3-4 miles at half-marathon race pace) Long run: 60-90 minutes at a comfortable pace Rest day
Week three: 80% mileage Easy run and strides Workout: Fartlek (30s hard, 60s easy, repeat for 20-30 minutes) Medium-long distance run Easy run and strides Workout: Track repeats (4x1600m at 10k race pace) Long run: 60-90 minutes at a comfortable pace Rest day
Week four: 85% mileage Easy run and strides Workout: Speed development (6x400m at slightly faster than mile race pace) Medium-long distance run Easy run and strides Workout: Interval training (8x600m at 5k race pace) Long run: 60-90 minutes at a comfortable pace Rest day
